Church of the Holy Sepulchre

It is one of the holiest sites in Christianity, believed to be the location of Jesus Christ's crucifixion, burial, and resurrection.

Ibrahimi Mosque

This is the Ibrahimi Mosque (or Cave of the Patriarchs) in Hebron. It is a significant religious site in Islam, Christianity, and Judaism, believed to be the burial place of the prophets Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob.

Church for all Nations

This is the Church of All Nations (also known as the Basilica of the Agony) in Jerusalem. It is located on the Mount of Olives and commemorates the site where Jesus prayed before his arrest.

Church of the Nativity

The Church of the Nativity is one of the oldest continuously inhabited Christian churches in the world and holds great significance as the traditional birthplace of Jesus Christ. Located in Bethlehem, West Bank, it is a major pilgrimage site for Christians and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Dome of the Rock

The Dome of the Rock is an iconic Islamic shrine located on the Temple Mount in Jerusalem. Completed in 691 CE, it is one of the oldest extant examples of Islamic architecture. The structure features a distinctive gold-plated dome, which rises prominently over the city skyline.

Al-Aqsa Mosque

Al-Aqsa Mosque, located in the Old City of Jerusalem, is considered the third holiest site in Islam after the Kaaba in Mecca and the Prophet's Mosque in Medina. It is believed to be the place where the Prophet Muhammad was transported during the Night Journey (Isra and Mi'raj) to the heavens. Before the Kaaba was designated as the qibla (direction of prayer), Muslims originally faced Al-Aqsa during their prayers.

The Great Omari Mosque

The Great Omari Mosque, located in Bethlehem, is a significant religious and historical site. Constructed in the 12th century during the Crusades, it was built on the site of a Byzantine church dedicated to St. Mary. Named after Caliph Umar ibn al-Khattab, who is known for his role in the early Islamic conquests, the mosque symbolizes the Islamic heritage of the region. Its architectural design features classic Islamic elements, including a spacious prayer hall and a prominent minaret, reflecting the styles typical of the era.
